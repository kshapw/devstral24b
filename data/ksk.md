# Karmika Seva Kendra (KSK) Knowledge Base

Karnataka Building & Other Construction Workers Welfare Board (KBOCWWB)

---

# Organization Overview

Karmika Seva Kendra (KSK) is an initiative of the Karnataka Building & Other Construction Workers Welfare Board (KBOCWWB). It provides construction workers and their families with access to registration, welfare schemes, claim assistance, and grievance support.

## Services Available

* Worker registration and ID card assistance
* Renewal and record updates
* Scheme application support
* Claim submission and tracking
* Guidance on worker rights
* Grievance support

## Mobile KSK (Sanchari Karmika Seva Kendra)

Mobile units travel to construction sites and rural areas to provide doorstep services including registration, scheme applications, and assistance.

# Website & General FAQs

Q: What is Karmika Seva Kendra?
A: It is a service center that helps construction workers register and apply for welfare schemes.

Q: Who can use the schemes?
A: Registered construction workers under KBOCWWB and eligible dependents.

Q: Can I apply online?
A: Yes, through the Board portal or Seva Sindhu.

Q: Where can I apply offline?
A: At KSK centers or Mobile KSK units.

Q: Is registration mandatory?
A: Yes, most schemes are available only for registered workers.

Q: What is the 90 days work certificate?
A: Proof that the worker has worked at least 90 days. Required for many schemes.

Q: Can dependents apply?
A: Yes, for schemes where dependents are eligible.

Q: What happens if documents are incorrect?
A: The application may be rejected or returned for correction.

## Scheme: Accident Assistance

### Overview

Financial assistance for death or permanent disability due to sudden accidents without criminal intent.

### Compensation

* Workplace death: ₹8,00,000
* Non-workplace death: ₹5,00,000
* Permanent total disability: ₹2,00,000
* Permanent partial disability: ₹1,00,000

### Eligibility

* Registered construction worker
* Accident occurred during employment or eligible conditions
* Application within 1 year

### Exclusions

* Natural death
* Suicide or self-harm
* Alcohol or drug influence
* Criminal activity
* Pregnancy-related cases
* HIV or sexually transmitted diseases

### Required Documents

* Medical certificate
* Disability certificate (if applicable)
* Death certificate
* FIR copy
* Postmortem report
* Self declaration
* 90 days work certificate

### FAQs

Q: How much compensation is given for accident death?
A: ₹8 lakh for workplace death and ₹5 lakh for non-workplace death.

Q: Is FIR mandatory?
A: Yes.

Q: Can I apply after one year?
A: No, applications must be submitted within one year.

Q: Is natural death covered?
A: No.

## Scheme: Assistance for Major Ailments

### Overview

Financial assistance up to ₹2,00,000 for treatment of major illnesses.

### Eligibility

* Registered worker
* Minimum 90 days work in last year
* Treatment for approved major ailment

### Required Documents

* Detailed medical report
* Discharge summary
* Form 22-A
* Self declaration
* 90 days work certificate

### FAQs

Q: What is the maximum benefit amount?
A: Up to ₹2,00,000.

Q: Can dependents apply?
A: Yes.

Q: Is hospital approval required?
A: Yes, treatment must be from a recognized hospital.

## Scheme: Thayi Magu Sahaya Hasta

### Overview

Support for child nutrition and pre-school care.

### Benefits

₹6,000 total (₹500 per month for 3 years)

### Eligibility

* Registered woman worker
* Applicable for first two children only

### Required Documents

* Birth certificate
* Child photo
* Affidavit
* Discharge summary
* Self declaration
* 90 days work certificate

### FAQs

Q: How long is the benefit provided?
A: For 3 years.

Q: Can I apply for more than two children?
A: No.

## Scheme: Delivery Assistance

### Overview

Financial assistance for childbirth.

### Benefit

₹50,000 per delivery (maximum two deliveries)

### Eligibility

* Registered woman worker
* Not eligible if already has two living children

### Required Documents

* Birth certificate
* Child photo
* Discharge summary
* Self declaration
* 90 days work certificate

### FAQs

Q: How many times can I claim?
A: Maximum two times.

## Scheme: Medical Assistance

### Overview

Hospitalization support.

### Benefit

₹300 per day (maximum ₹20,000)

### Eligibility

Minimum 48 hours hospitalization.

### Required Documents

* Discharge summary
* Form 22A
* Treatment bills
* Self declaration
* 90 days work certificate

### FAQs

Q: Can private hospital treatment be covered?
A: Yes, if empaneled or recognized.

## Scheme: Disability Pension & Ex-Gratia

### Overview

Monthly pension and one-time assistance for disability.

### Benefits

* Pension: ₹2,000/month
* Ex-gratia: Up to ₹2,00,000

### Eligibility

Certified partial disability.

### Required Documents

* Disability certificate
* Medical records
* Self declaration
* 90 days work certificate

### FAQs

Q: When does the pension stop?
A: At age 60.

Q: Is yearly verification required?
A: Yes, Living Certificate must be submitted annually.

---

## Scheme: Continuation of Disability Pension

### Overview

Continuation of existing disability pension.

### Eligibility

* Previously sanctioned pension
* Age below 60

### Requirement

Annual Living Certificate submission.

---

## Scheme: Pension

### Overview

Monthly pension for workers aged 60 or above.

### Benefit

Up to ₹3,000 per month.

### Eligibility

* Age 60+
* Registered beneficiary for at least 3 years

### Required Documents

* Self declaration
* 90 days work certificate
* Living Certificate

### FAQs

Q: Can I receive other government pension along with this?
A: No.

---

## Scheme: Continuation of Pension

### Overview

Continuation of pension after retirement.

### Requirements

* Annual Living Certificate
* No overlap with other pension schemes

---

## Scheme: Funeral and Ex-Gratia

### Overview

Financial support to family after death of a worker.

### Benefits

* Funeral expenses: ₹4,000
* Ex-gratia: ₹1,46,000

### Required Documents

* Death certificate
* Nominee Aadhaar

### FAQs

Q: Who can apply?
A: Nominee or legal heir.

---

## Scheme: Marriage Assistance

### Overview

Financial support for marriage of worker’s children.

### Benefit

₹60,000 per marriage (maximum two claims per family)

### Eligibility

Worker registered for at least 1 year.

### Required Documents

* Marriage certificate
* Affidavit
* Self declaration
* 90 days work certificate

### FAQs

Q: Can I apply twice?
A: Yes, maximum two times per family.

Q: Is duplicate claim allowed for same child?
A: No.

# Common User Questions (High-Frequency)

Q: Which schemes require 90 days work certificate?
A: Most schemes including medical, accident, pension, and assistance schemes.

Q: How do I check application status?
A: Through the portal or by visiting KSK.

Q: Where can I get help filling the application?
A: At KSK centers or Mobile KSK units.

Q: Are all schemes available for dependents?
A: Only schemes that explicitly allow dependents.
